In summary...

  • We've seen our first peak, due to social isolation policies. That can lull us into complacency, so it's likely to see new peaks in the summer and in the fall.
  • Testing in Asia, the quality of testing is way above what we see in the US. Our testing numbers should never be compared, due to testing prioritization.
  • On "megatesting" -- can we get to 20M tests a day? Not realistically, from a logistics point of view.
  • The quoted 200K tests / day is a bogus number, because it's not prioritized -- high-income individuals in the US gets tested more frequently than others which skews the results.
  • There are a number of individuals with a very high viral load that are pre-symptomatic or never-symptomatic. That poses a lot of risk.
